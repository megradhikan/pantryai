# Product

## Register

product

## Users

College students managing a small pantry on a budget. They cook occasionally, want to stretch what they buy, and hate throwing food away. Primary context: quick checks on their phone — scanning a receipt after a grocery run, glancing at what's expiring before heading to the kitchen, pulling up a recipe while cooking. Secondary context: browsing recipes and building a shopping list while sitting in class or on the couch.

## Product Purpose

PantryAI turns a grocery receipt into a living inventory. It tracks what you have, warns when things expire, suggests recipes from what's already in your pantry, and builds a shopping list for anything missing. The goal: less food waste, less decision fatigue about what to eat, and fewer unnecessary grocery trips. Success looks like a student opening the app, seeing three things are expiring tomorrow, picking a recipe that uses all three, and walking away having cooked instead of wasted.

## Brand Personality

Warm, friendly, approachable. The app should feel like a knowledgeable friend who happens to know what's in your fridge — not a dashboard, not a chore tracker. Rounded, natural, inviting. It draws from the tactile warmth of a well-used recipe book, not the clinical precision of a spreadsheet.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS dashboards: no corporate blues, no metric hero cards, no "enterprise" feel. This is a personal tool, not a B2B product.
- Cluttered grocery apps (Instacart, Flipp): no overwhelming category grids, tiny text, or information overload.
- Gamified apps: no streaks, badges, or confetti. The motivation is practical (save money, eat better), not performative.

## Design Principles

1. **Kitchen-counter ready.** Every screen should be usable one-handed while holding a spatula. Big tap targets, clear hierarchy, no precision interactions.
2. **Show what matters now.** Lead with urgency — what's expiring, what you can cook tonight. History and stats are secondary.
3. **Reduce, don't add.** Each feature should eliminate a decision, not create one. Fewer choices, smarter defaults, less cognitive load.
4. **Honest and direct.** No marketing language in the UI. "3 items expire tomorrow" beats "Optimize your pantry freshness score."
5. **Delight through care, not decoration.** The satisfaction comes from the app being genuinely useful — knowing what you have, not having to think about it. Not from animations or visual tricks.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- Good contrast ratios (4.5:1 body text minimum)
- Readable text sizes (no text below 13px)
- Reduced motion support for animations
- Touch targets minimum 44px
